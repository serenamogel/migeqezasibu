Super Bowl Champion Matt Ulrich Passes Away at Age 41

Matt Ulrich, the former linebacker for the Indianapolis Colts who helped the team win Super Bowl XLI, has died at the age of 41. Ulrich passed away on November 8th, 2023 in his hometown of Bozeman, Montana. The news of his untimely death has shocked the NFL community.

Ulrich’s Football Career

Ulrich played college football at Montana State before being drafted by the Colts in 1998. He went on to spend his entire 9 year NFL career with the Colts, being part of the franchise’s golden era in the 2000s under quarterback Peyton Manning.

Ulrich was a key member of the Colts defense that won Super Bowl XLI in 2007, defeating the Chicago Bears. He retired after the 2006 season due to injury problems.

Reaction from Former Teammates

Several of Ulrich’s former Colts teammates expressed their sadness at the news of his passing. Peyton Manning called Ulrich “a true competitor and a great teammate.” Defensive end Dwight Freeney said “the Super Bowl doesn’t happen without Matt.”

Jim Caldwell, who was the Colts head coach for Super Bowl XLI, stated “Matt played the game with passion and was beloved by coaches and players alike.”

How did Matt Ulrich Died? Cause of Death

The cause of Matt Ulrich’s death has not been officially released. Some reports indicate it may have been suicide, but this remains unconfirmed. Police in Bozeman continue to investigate the circumstances surrounding Ulrich’s passing.

Regardless of the cause, the premature death of this Super Bowl champion has left many in shock and mourning the loss of a great football talent. He will be remembered fondly by Colts fans as a key contributor during the team’s greatest era.
