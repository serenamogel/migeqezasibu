Min Hyo-rin Biography

Min Hyo-rin Career

Jung Eun-ran, her birth name from Daegu, became Min Hyo-rin when she started modelling for the apparel company Flapper in 2006.

Later, she had multiple appearances in music videos like F.T. Island and Park Ki-young. Large teaser posters of her were put up across Seoul’s hotspots before to her singing debut as a marketing ploy. In 2007 and 2008, she released the albums RinZ and Touch Me, respectively.

With Lee Jung-Jae and Song Joong-ki, Min made her acting debut in the 2009 television series Triple. She played the main role in a 2010 web series based on the book Romantic Movement by Alain de Botton, which was directed by four different filmmakers and shot in various parts of Seoul.

In the reality series Fox’s Butler, singer Seven and presenter Min were partnered, and from 2010 to 2011, Min presented the sixth season of the cable channel Mnet’s Trend Report Feel.

She and Choi Daniel appeared in the 2011 short film Age of Milk, which was produced on a Samsung Galaxy S smartphone and shown on the cable network OCN.

Later the same year, she was cast with Sung Yu-ri and Kim Min-jun in the TV show Romance Town (#11), and she also had an appearance in Song Jieun’s “Going Crazy” music video.
