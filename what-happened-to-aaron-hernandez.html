What Happened To Aaron Hernandez?

The story of Aaron Hernandez is a tragic tale of a promising athlete whose life spiraled out of control, leading to a series of criminal acts and ultimately his death. Hernandez was a talented football player who played as a tight end for the New England Patriots in the National Football League (NFL). His career was cut short by his arrest and subsequent conviction for murder. This article delves into the life of Aaron Hernandez, the events that led to his downfall, and the aftermath of his actions.

Early Life and Football Career

Aaron Hernandez was born on November 6, 1989, in Bristol, Connecticut. He was a standout athlete in high school and was heavily recruited by colleges across the country. Hernandez chose to attend the University of Florida, where he had a successful college football career and was part of the team that won the 2009 BCS National Championship.

  • High school football star at Bristol Central High School
  • Recruited by the University of Florida
  • Won the John Mackey Award in 2009 as the nation’s best tight end

Transition to the NFL

Despite his success in college, concerns about Hernandez’s behavior off the field caused his draft stock to fall. He was eventually selected by the New England Patriots in the fourth round of the 2010 NFL Draft.

  • Drafted by the New England Patriots in 2010
  • Received a five-year contract extension in 2012
  • Played alongside Rob Gronkowski, forming a dynamic tight end duo

The Downfall Begins

While Hernandez’s football career was progressing, his personal life was troubled. He was linked to several incidents that suggested a pattern of violent behavior.

  • Allegations of failed drug tests and links to violent incidents in college
  • Connections to individuals with criminal backgrounds

The Odin Lloyd Murder Case

In June 2013, the body of Odin Lloyd, a semi-professional football player who was dating the sister of Hernandez’s fiancée, was found shot to death. Hernandez quickly became the prime suspect in the murder investigation.

  • Hernandez was arrested and charged with first-degree murder
  • Released by the New England Patriots hours after his arrest
  • Trial began in January 2015
  • Convicted of first-degree murder in April 2015
  • Sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole

Other Legal Troubles

Beyond the Odin Lloyd case, Hernandez was implicated in other criminal activities, including a double homicide in Boston in 2012. He was acquitted of these charges in a separate trial in 2017.

  • Charged with the 2012 double homicide of Daniel de Abreu and Safiro Furtado
  • Acquitted of the double murder charges in April 2017

The Death of Aaron Hernandez

Just days after his acquittal for the double homicide, Aaron Hernandez was found dead in his prison cell. His death was ruled a suicide.

  • Found dead on April 19, 2017
  • Death ruled a suicide by hanging
  • Left behind notes for his fiancée, daughter, and legal team

Aftermath and Legacy

The death of Aaron Hernandez left many unanswered questions and prompted discussions about mental health, chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE), and the culture of football.

  • Discussion about the impact of CTE on Hernandez’s behavior
  • Legal battles over his conviction and the abatement ab initio legal principle
  • Netflix documentary “Killer Inside: The Mind of Aaron Hernandez” released in 2020

CTE Diagnosis

After his death, Hernandez’s brain was examined for signs of CTE, a degenerative brain disease found in individuals with a history of repetitive brain trauma, including many football players.

  • Diagnosed with Stage 3 CTE, which can cause violent mood swings and other cognitive disorders
  • CTE diagnosis raised questions about the NFL’s responsibility for player safety

Impact on the NFL

The Hernandez case brought renewed scrutiny to the NFL’s handling of off-field behavior and player health issues, particularly related to head injuries.

  • NFL’s personal conduct policy and disciplinary measures questioned
  • Increased focus on concussion protocols and player safety

Financial and Legal Ramifications

Hernandez’s arrest and conviction had significant financial implications for both him and the New England Patriots.

  • Patriots voided Hernandez’s contract, including guaranteed money
  • Lawsuits filed by the families of Odin Lloyd and the victims of the 2012 double homicide

Personal Life and Relationships

Hernandez’s personal relationships, particularly with his fiancée Shayanna Jenkins and their daughter, were a focal point during his trials and after his death.

  • Engaged to Shayanna Jenkins, with whom he had a daughter
  • Jenkins stood by Hernandez throughout his legal troubles

Media Coverage and Public Perception

The intense media coverage of Hernandez’s life, trials, and death shaped public perception of the former NFL player.

  • Widespread media coverage of the murder trial and other legal proceedings
  • Public fascination with Hernandez’s fall from grace

Conclusion

The story of Aaron Hernandez is a cautionary tale about the potential consequences of unchecked behavior, the importance of mental health awareness, and the impact of contact sports on athletes. His life and death continue to be a subject of discussion and analysis, as society grapples with the complex issues raised by his case.

FAQ Section

What was Aaron Hernandez convicted of?

Aaron Hernandez was convicted of the first-degree murder of Odin Lloyd in April 2015.

Did Aaron Hernandez have CTE?

Yes, after his death, an examination of Hernandez’s brain revealed that he had Stage 3 CTE.

What happened to Aaron Hernandez’s NFL career?

Aaron Hernandez’s NFL career ended abruptly after his arrest for the murder of Odin Lloyd. The New England Patriots released him, and he never returned to professional football.
