Janice Dean FOX News Meteorologist

Janice Dean became a part of FOX News Channel (FNC) in January 2004, and presently, she holds the position of senior meteorologist. Additionally, she serves as the morning meteorologist for FNC’s flagship morning program, FOX & Friends, airing on weekdays from 6 to 9 AM/ET. Janice also contributes to FOX Weather, which is FOX News Media’s ad-supported streaming television (“FAST”) weather service. READ: Geoff Bennett

Throughout her tenure with the network, Dean has extensively covered various destructive storms, including Hurricanes Harvey, Irma, Sandy, Irene, Igor, Earl, and Katrina. Her most recent contribution involved coverage of Hurricane Ida in 2021.

Beyond her responsibilities at FNC, Janice Dean is a successful author. She created the popular children’s series “Freddy the Frogcaster,” designed to educate children about the weather. Notably, she generously donated all earnings from her latest “Freddy” title to Team Rubicon, an organization committed to providing disaster relief to those affected by natural disasters. In 2019, Dean released her memoir, titled “Mostly Sunny: How I Learned to Keep Smiling Through the Rainiest Days,” followed by “Make Your Own Sunshine: Inspiring Stories of People Who Find Light in Dark Times” in 2021.

Before her association with FOX News, Dean served as the news editor and entertainment reporter for “Imus in the Morning,” which aired on WFAN-AM New York and was nationally simulcast on MSNBC. Prior to her radio stint, she worked as a weekend on-air traffic reporter for CBS 2 New York. From 1998 to 2001, Dean hosted various radio programs in Houston, Texas, including Metro Networks, KODA-FM, KKBQ-FM, and KLDE-FM. In 1997, she also served as an on-camera weather host at CBOT Television.

Dean initiated her career in Ottawa, Canada, at CHEZ-FM, where she co-hosted a morning show, and worked as a reporter and DJ. She later held multiple roles at CHUM Limited in Ottawa, such as morning news anchor for “Breakfast at the New RO,” producer and host of “The Broad Perspective” on CFRA, and an anchor for KOOL-FM.
